Beneath the soft golden hue of the setting sun, the quiet town buzzed with an unusual stillness. Leaves rustled gently in the breeze, carrying with them the scent of fresh rain and blooming jasmine. A cat lounged lazily on a sun-warmed wall, its eyes half-closed in contentment. Somewhere in the distance, a child’s laughter echoed through narrow alleyways, weaving its way between homes painted in fading pastels. It was one of those rare evenings where time seemed to pause, allowing everyone and everything to simply exist — calm, unhurried, and deeply present.

The old lighthouse stood tall at the edge of the cliff, its weathered stones bearing silent witness to countless storms. Waves crashed violently against the rocks below, sending salty spray into the wind. Inside, the air smelled of oil and time, and the slow, rhythmic turning of the light created a comforting hum. Though no longer manned, the beacon still flashed dutifully each night, a relic of a bygone era offering quiet guidance to distant ships. To the few who wandered near, it felt like a guardian of forgotten stories, whispering tales of shipwrecks, sailors, and secrets lost to the sea.
